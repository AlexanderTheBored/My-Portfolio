Lesson 1 Data • Information • Knowledge

Lesson 1: Data, Information, and Knowledge

Core concepts • simple reviewer

Key definitions

Short, exam-ready explanations in my own words

Concepts
  • Data — raw facts or figures with no immediate meaning yet (e.g., numbers, dates, names).
  • Information — data that has been processed and given context so it answers a question or supports a decision.
  • Knowledge — information combined with experience, understanding, and judgment; what a person or organization “knows” and can apply.

Simple example

How data turns into information, then knowledge

Example
  • Data: “5, 7, 9, 3, 8”
  • Information: “These are quiz scores of one student in ITC.”
  • Knowledge: “The student is consistently passing but might need review on topics where the score drops.”
